# Simulated Asset Management — Board Meeting Summary

**Document Type**: Board Meeting Minutes Summary  
**Meeting Date**: Q4 2024 Board Meeting  
**Classification**: Confidential — Board Use Only  
**Prepared By**: Company Secretary

---

## Meeting Overview

The Board of Directors of Simulated Asset Management convened for the Q4 2024 quarterly meeting to review firm performance, strategic initiatives, and governance matters. All directors were present, with the Chief Executive Officer, Chief Investment Officer, and Chief Financial Officer providing management presentations.

---

## Business Performance Review

**Assets Under Management**: Total AUM reached £12.5 billion at quarter-end, representing 15% year-over-year growth. Net inflows of £2.1 billion for the year exceeded the £1.5 billion target, driven by strong demand for sustainable investment strategies and positive investment performance.

**Investment Performance**: 75% of firm AUM is in strategies outperforming their benchmarks over three years. Notable performers include the ESG Leaders Global Equity strategy (+14.5% vs benchmark +13.1%) and the Technology & Infrastructure strategy (+18.2% vs benchmark +15.8%). The Multi-Asset Income strategy underperformed modestly due to fixed income positioning.

**Financial Results**: Revenue increased 12% to £78 million, with operating margin improving to 35% from 33% in the prior year. Cost discipline and scale benefits contributed to margin expansion. Performance fees of £4.2 million reflected strong absolute returns in growth strategies.

**Client Activity**: Client retention remained strong at 96%. New mandate wins totalled £1.8 billion, including a significant £400 million ESG mandate from a European pension fund. The sales pipeline remains healthy with £2.5 billion in advanced discussions.

---

## Strategic Initiative Updates

**Sustainable Investment Expansion**: The Board reviewed progress on sustainable investment initiatives. ESG-focused AUM grew 25% to £3.2 billion, now representing 26% of total firm assets. Two new sustainable strategies launched successfully, with combined AUM of £450 million. The Board approved continued investment in ESG research capabilities.

**Technology Modernisation**: The technology transformation programme remains on track and within budget. Cloud migration for core investment systems achieved 60% completion. The new client portal launched in Q3 with positive client feedback. Full programme completion expected by Q2 2025.

**Distribution Expansion**: European distribution initiatives progressed with establishment of Swiss representative office. Initial client meetings in Germany and Switzerland generated positive interest. The Board approved expansion of the Continental European sales team.

---

## Risk and Compliance Matters

**Risk Dashboard Review**: The Chief Risk Officer presented the quarterly risk dashboard. All risk metrics remained within established limits. Market risk exposure was modestly reduced through defensive positioning in equity strategies. Operational risk incidents declined 30% year-over-year following control enhancements.

**Regulatory Updates**: The Board received updates on regulatory developments including FCA sustainability disclosure requirements and EU SFDR implementation. Management confirmed readiness for upcoming regulatory changes. No material compliance issues were identified during the quarter.

**Cybersecurity**: The technology team completed annual penetration testing with no critical vulnerabilities identified. Enhanced security monitoring implemented following industry threat intelligence. Employee security awareness training completed with 100% participation.

---

## M&A Discussion

The Board held an executive session to discuss potential acquisition opportunities. Management presented analysis of two targets:

**Target A — European Sustainable Equity Boutique**: €2 billion AUM sustainable equity specialist with strong performance track record. Strategic fit with ESG growth priorities. Preliminary valuation discussions indicate 2.5% of AUM consideration. The Board authorised management to proceed with detailed due diligence.

**Target B — Asian Fixed Income Specialist**: €1.5 billion AUM fixed income manager focused on Asian credit markets. Would provide geographic and product diversification. Integration complexity higher due to operational differences. The Board requested additional analysis before proceeding.

**M&A Criteria Confirmation**: The Board reaffirmed acquisition criteria including strategic fit, cultural compatibility, financial discipline, and integration feasibility. Maximum consideration of 3% of target AUM confirmed as valuation guideline.

---

## Governance Matters

**Board Composition**: The Nomination Committee reported on board succession planning. Current board composition meets diversity and independence requirements. Search initiated for additional non-executive director with technology industry expertise to support digital transformation oversight.

**Executive Compensation**: The Remuneration Committee presented preliminary 2024 bonus recommendations based on firm performance, investment results, and individual objectives. Final recommendations to be approved at February 2025 meeting following year-end audit completion.

**Committee Reports**: Audit Committee confirmed clean quarterly review with external auditors. Risk Committee approved updated risk appetite statement. Investment Committee reviewed investment process enhancements.

---

## Capital and Dividend Matters

**Capital Position**: The firm maintains strong capital position with regulatory capital surplus of £15 million above minimum requirements. Balance sheet remains conservatively managed with no debt financing.

**Dividend Recommendation**: Based on strong financial performance and capital position, management recommended interim dividend of £0.45 per share, representing 50% payout ratio. The Board approved the dividend recommendation for payment in January 2025.

**Share Buyback**: The Board discussed potential share buyback programme to return excess capital. Decision deferred pending clarity on M&A capital requirements and 2025 strategic investment needs.

---

The Board passed the following resolutions:

2. Authorised management to proceed with Target A due diligence
3. Approved interim dividend of £0.45 per share
4. Approved updated risk appetite statement
5. Authorised European distribution team expansion

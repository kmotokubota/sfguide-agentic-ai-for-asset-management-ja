# Client Retention Playbook

**Simulated Asset Management**  
**At-Risk Client Engagement and Retention Strategies**

---

## Template Purpose

This playbook provides structured guidance for relationship managers when engaging with at-risk clients showing signs of potential redemption. It includes early warning indicators, engagement strategies, retention tactics, and escalation procedures to maximize client retention.

**Audience**: Relationship Managers, Client Service Teams, Senior Leadership

---

## Section 1: Early Warning Indicators

### Quantitative Warning Signs

| Indicator | Warning Level | Action Trigger |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| Net redemptions 2+ consecutive months | Amber | Proactive outreach |
| Net redemptions >10% of AUM in quarter | Red | Immediate engagement |
| Reduced meeting participation | Amber | Review relationship |
| Delayed response to communications | Amber | Escalate contact frequency |
| New RFP activity detected | Red | Senior leadership involvement |
| Change in key client contacts | Amber | Relationship reset meeting |

### Qualitative Warning Signs

- Increased questions about fees or value-for-money
- Requests for competitive analysis or benchmarking
- New consultant involvement or manager review announcement
- Changes in client organization (new CIO, board changes)
- Reduced engagement in quarterly reviews
- Reluctance to commit to forward calendar

### Risk Scoring Framework

| Score | Risk Level | Description | Response Timeframe |
|-------|------------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1-3 | Low | Normal relationship patterns | Standard service |
| 4-6 | Medium | Some concerning indicators | Enhanced attention |
| 7-8 | High | Multiple warning signs | Immediate action plan |
| 9-10 | Critical | Imminent redemption risk | Executive escalation |

## Section 2: Engagement Strategy

### Initial Assessment

Before engaging with an at-risk client, complete this assessment:

**Relationship Health Check**

- [ ] Review last 12 months of client interactions
- [ ] Analyze flow history and AUM trend
- [ ] Review performance vs benchmark and peer group
- [ ] Assess service delivery quality
- [ ] Identify any unresolved issues or complaints
- [ ] Evaluate competitive positioning

**Client Context Analysis**

- [ ] Any organizational changes at client?
- [ ] New investment committee members?
- [ ] Change in consultant relationship?
- [ ] Shifts in investment policy or allocation?
- [ ] Budget pressures or fee sensitivity?
- [ ] Industry/peer group trends?

### Engagement Principles

1. **Act Early**: The earlier intervention begins, the higher success rate
2. **Listen First**: Understand concerns before proposing solutions
3. **Be Transparent**: Acknowledge performance issues honestly
4. **Add Value**: Demonstrate ongoing value beyond performance
5. **Involve Leadership**: Senior presence signals client importance
6. **Document Everything**: Track all interactions and commitments

## Section 3: Retention Conversation Framework

### Opening the Dialogue

*[Use when initiating retention-focused conversation]*

**Approach**: Proactive, consultative, non-defensive

**Sample Opening**:
"[Client Name], I wanted to schedule time to discuss our partnership and ensure we're fully aligned with your evolving needs. We value this relationship deeply and want to ensure we're delivering maximum value. I'd like to understand any concerns and discuss how we can better serve you."

### Discussion Framework

**1. Listen and Acknowledge**

- "Help me understand your perspective on our performance..."
- "What aspects of our service have been most valuable?"
- "Where do you feel we could improve?"
- "What changes in your organization should we be aware of?"

**2. Address Performance Concerns** (if applicable)

- Present performance in proper context (market environment, benchmark appropriateness)
- Acknowledge underperformance periods honestly
- Explain positioning rationale and forward view
- Provide peer comparison where favourable
- Discuss any process enhancements implemented

**3. Demonstrate Value**

- Highlight service quality and responsiveness
- Reference ESG integration and stewardship activities
- Discuss thought leadership and market insights provided
- Note operational excellence and reporting quality
- Quantify total value of relationship (beyond fees)

**4. Explore Solutions**

- "What would success look like for our partnership going forward?"
- "Are there additional services or reporting that would add value?"
- "Would adjusted fee arrangements help address your concerns?"
- "How can we better integrate with your investment team?"

**5. Secure Commitment**

- Agree on specific action items with timelines
- Schedule follow-up meeting to review progress
- Confirm ongoing communication preferences
- Document all commitments in writing

## Section 4: Retention Tactics

### Service Enhancement Options

| Tactic | Description | When to Deploy |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Enhanced Reporting | Custom reports, increased frequency | Value perception issues |
| PM Access | Direct portfolio manager engagement | Investment concerns |
| Thought Leadership | Custom research, exclusive insights | Differentiation needs |
| Governance Support | Board/committee presentations | Stakeholder education |
| ESG Deep Dive | Enhanced sustainability reporting | ESG-focused clients |
| Strategic Reviews | Annual strategic planning sessions | Long-term alignment |

### Fee and Structure Options

*[Requires senior leadership approval]*

| Option | Description | Approval Level |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Fee Review | Competitive analysis and potential adjustment | Head of Client Relations |
| Performance Fee | Shift to performance-aligned structure | CIO + Head of Sales |
| Volume Discount | Additional mandate fee reduction | Head of Sales |
| Fee Holiday | Temporary fee reduction period | CEO |

**Note**: Fee concessions should be last resort. Focus first on value demonstration and service enhancement.

### Relationship Intensification

- Increase meeting frequency (quarterly to monthly)
- Add senior leadership touchpoints
- Create custom market commentary
- Provide early access to investment team views
- Invite to exclusive client events
- Offer site visits and team introductions

## Section 5: Escalation Procedures

### When to Escalate

Escalate to senior leadership when:

- Client explicitly mentions RFP or manager review
- Redemption notice received
- Client requests fee concession
- Consultant recommendation against SAM
- Risk score reaches 8+
- Relationship Manager unable to secure meeting

### Escalation Path

| Risk Level | Primary Owner | Escalation To | Response SLA |
|------------|---------------|---------------|--------------|
| Medium (4-6) | Relationship Manager | Head of Client Relations | 48 hours |
| High (7-8) | Head of Client Relations | Head of Sales | 24 hours |
| Critical (9-10) | Head of Sales | CEO + CIO | Same day |

### Executive Engagement Protocol

When senior leadership is involved:

1. **Briefing Document**: Prepare comprehensive client summary
2. **Strategy Alignment**: Agree on messaging and offers
3. **Joint Approach**: RM and executive together
4. **Follow-up Ownership**: Clear accountability for actions
5. **Board Reporting**: Update leadership on outcome

## Section 6: Win-Back Strategy

### If Client Provides Redemption Notice

**Immediate Actions** (within 24 hours):

- Acknowledge receipt professionally
- Request meeting to discuss transition
- Escalate to senior leadership
- Prepare retention counter-proposal

**During Notice Period**:

- Maintain service excellence
- Complete all reporting obligations
- Offer orderly transition support
- Keep communication lines open
- Document exit feedback

**Post-Departure**:

- Conduct exit interview
- Maintain relationship with contacts
- Add to win-back pipeline
- Schedule 6-month check-in
- Track competitor performance

### Win-Back Approach

*[For former clients who departed in last 24 months]*

- Monitor public performance of replacement manager
- Maintain periodic contact (quarterly market updates)
- Offer to re-present after 12+ months
- Leverage any organizational changes (new CIO)
- Prepare "what's changed" presentation
